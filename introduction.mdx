Capisum is a unified API layer that lets your application connect to payment gateways, digital banks, wallets, crypto platforms, and account infrastructure providers through one standard integration. Instead of writing and maintaining a separate integration for every provider you want to support, you send one request shape to Capisum, and Capisum handles the translation, routing, and normalization behind the scenes — returning a single, consistent response no matter which provider executed the operation.

## What Capisum does

When your app sends a request to Capisum, the platform validates the payload, routes it to the provider you specified, and returns a normalized response. Every step in that flow — from request fields to error codes to response structure — follows the same standard across all 30+ supported providers.

Here is how a transaction flows through the system:

1. Your app sends `POST /v1/transactions` with a provider, amount, currency, and customer.
2. Capisum validates and normalizes the request.
3. Capisum routes it to the selected provider via that provider's adapter.
4. The provider processes the transaction and returns its native response.
5. Capisum normalizes that response and returns one standard payload to your app.

Your application code never has to parse a Paystack response differently from a Flutterwave response. The status, fields, and error vocabulary are always the same.

## What Capisum is not

<Warning>
  Capisum does not process payments directly and does not replace your underlying providers. You still need active accounts and credentials with any provider you want to use. Capisum standardizes how your application connects to those providers — it does not sit between your customers and the money movement itself.
</Warning>

Capisum also does not handle KYC, KYB, or compliance on your behalf. Those responsibilities remain between you and each provider. Capisum's role is integration, standardization, routing, and observability.

## Provider categories

Capisum organizes its 30+ adapters into four categories. You can connect providers from multiple categories under a single Capisum account and switch between them using the same API call shape.

| Category | Examples | Operations |
|---|---|---|
| Payment gateways | Paystack, Flutterwave, Monnify, Korapay, SeerBit | Pay-in, Payout |
| Digital banks & wallets | Kuda, OPay, PalmPay, Moniepoint, Paga | Wallet, Pay-in, Payout |
| Crypto platforms | Quidax, Binance, Luno, Yellow Card, Busha | Crypto, Settlement, Pay-in |
| Account infrastructure | Mono, Anchor, Bridge, Okra, Maplerad | Accounts, Data, Stablecoins |

## Sandbox environment

Capisum includes a sandbox mode so you can test pay-in, payout, and account flows without production credentials or real money movement. Use your `cap_sk_test_...` key to run against the sandbox. Everything behaves identically to production — the same request shapes, the same response fields, and the same error codes — so promoting your integration from test to live requires only a key swap.

<Note>
  The sandbox is available to all Capisum accounts at no extra cost. Test as much as you need before going live.
</Note>

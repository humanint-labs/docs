Nigeria's digital banking and mobile wallet ecosystem operates across a distinct set of rails from traditional card-based gateways. OPay, PalmPay, Kuda, and Moniepoint each have their own APIs, auth patterns, and response formats — but through Capisum, you interact with all of them using the same request shape. This category is particularly useful if your product needs to accept payments from or disburse funds to customers who primarily transact through wallet-first apps rather than traditional bank accounts.

<Note>
  Capisum does not hold wallet balances or replace your provider accounts. You need active credentials with each digital bank or wallet provider you want to use. Connect them under **Settings → Providers** in the Capisum dashboard.
</Note>

## Unified request shape

The `provider` field in your request determines which wallet or digital bank handles the transaction. Everything else — the amount, currency, customer object, and reference — stays the same.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="TypeScript">
    ```typescript
    // Pay via Kuda wallet
    const transaction = await capisum.payments.create({
      provider: "kuda",
      type: "pay_in",
      amount: 100000,
      currency: "NGN",
      customer: { email: "customer@example.com" },
      reference: "wallet-pay-001",
    });
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="cURL">
    ```bash
    curl -X POST https://api.capisum.com/v1/transactions \
      -H "Authorization: Bearer <YOUR_API_KEY>" \
      -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
      -d '{
        "provider": "kuda",
        "type": "pay_in",
        "amount": 100000,
        "currency": "NGN",
        "customer": { "email": "customer@example.com" },
        "reference": "wallet-pay-001"
      }'
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Live providers

These providers are production-ready. Their adapters are fully implemented and available for live traffic.

---

### OPay **Live**

Connect wallet and digital banking payment flows via OPay, one of Nigeria's largest super-app wallets with a broad consumer and merchant user base.

**Supported operations:** Wallet, Payout

---

### PalmPay **Live**

Route mobile wallet top-ups and merchant payments through PalmPay. PalmPay has grown rapidly in the Nigerian mass-market segment and supports merchant collections natively.

**Supported operations:** Wallet, Pay-in

---

### Kuda **Live**

Route digital bank transactions through one API. Kuda Bank operates as a fully licensed microfinance bank and offers developer-accessible APIs for transfers and collections.

**Supported operations:** Wallet, Pay-in, Payout

---

## Beta providers

These providers are available now with limited testing. You can connect and build against them, but validate thoroughly before sending high-volume production traffic.

---

### Moniepoint **Beta**

Connect agent banking and wallet settlement workflows. Moniepoint has significant reach in offline and agent-banking contexts across Nigeria and is widely used for last-mile disbursements.

**Supported operations:** Wallet, Payout

---

### Paga **Beta**

Link mobile money transfers and bill payment flows. Paga is one of Nigeria's pioneering mobile money platforms and supports both consumer transfers and business-facing collections.

**Supported operations:** Wallet, Pay-in

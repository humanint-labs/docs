Account infrastructure providers sit at a different layer from payment gateways and wallets. They power the underlying financial data and account primitives that your product needs — open banking data retrieval, embedded account provisioning, virtual USD account issuance, stablecoin settlement rails, and pay-by-bank payment initiation. Capisum wraps these providers behind the same unified API surface, so you can combine account data from Mono, provisioned accounts from Anchor, and stablecoin infrastructure from Bridge without building separate integrations for each.

<Note>
  Each account infrastructure provider has its own onboarding, compliance, and credential requirements. You need an active account with each provider before you can connect it in Capisum. Add your credentials under **Settings → Providers** in the Capisum dashboard.
</Note>

## Use cases

Account infrastructure providers enable four distinct categories of functionality within Capisum:

- **Open banking data retrieval** — Link customer bank accounts and retrieve transaction history, balances, and account metadata. Useful for underwriting, reconciliation, and financial management products. (Mono, Okra)
- **Embedded account provisioning** — Create and manage bank accounts, sub-accounts, or virtual accounts on behalf of your users directly from your application. (Anchor, Maplerad, Unit)
- **Stablecoin settlement** — Route settlements over stablecoin rails as an alternative to traditional fiat transfer infrastructure. (Bridge)
- **Virtual account issuance** — Issue virtual USD-denominated accounts or cards for business customers. (Maplerad)
- **Pay-by-bank** — Initiate account-to-account payments directly from a linked bank account, bypassing card networks. (Okra, Stitch)

## Unified request shape

Account infrastructure operations use the same request structure as other Capisum categories. The `provider` field identifies the infrastructure provider, and the `type` field maps to the operation being performed.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="TypeScript">
    ```typescript
    // Retrieve account data via Mono
    const transaction = await capisum.payments.create({
      provider: "mono",
      type: "pay_in",
      amount: 200000,
      currency: "NGN",
      customer: { email: "customer@example.com" },
      reference: "account-link-001",
    });
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="cURL">
    ```bash
    curl -X POST https://api.capisum.com/v1/transactions \
      -H "Authorization: Bearer <YOUR_API_KEY>" \
      -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
      -d '{
        "provider": "mono",
        "type": "pay_in",
        "amount": 200000,
        "currency": "NGN",
        "customer": { "email": "customer@example.com" },
        "reference": "account-link-001"
      }'
    ```
  </Tab>

## Beta providers

These providers are available now and can be connected to your application. Adapters are still receiving improvements — validate thoroughly before using in high-volume production workflows.

---

### Bridge **Beta**

Access account and stablecoin infrastructure. Bridge enables stablecoin-native settlement and account provisioning, useful for teams building products that need to move value across fiat and crypto-adjacent rails.

**Supported operations:** Accounts, Stablecoins

---

### Mono **Beta**

Connect financial account data and bank account workflows. Mono provides open banking APIs that let your application link to a customer's bank account to retrieve balances, statements, and transaction history with customer consent.

**Supported operations:** Data, Accounts

---

### Okra **Beta**

Link open banking data and payment initiation. Okra goes beyond read-only data — it also supports pay-by-bank payment initiation, allowing you to trigger account-to-account transfers from a linked bank account.

**Supported operations:** Data, Pay-in

---

### Anchor **Beta**

Provision business banking accounts and transfers. Anchor is a banking-as-a-service platform that lets you create and manage NGN bank accounts, sub-wallets, and transfer flows programmatically.

**Supported operations:** Accounts, Payout

---

### Maplerad **Beta**

Connect virtual USD accounts and card issuance. Maplerad lets you provision USD-denominated virtual accounts and issue cards to your users, which is useful for multi-currency products or cross-border business banking.

**Supported operations:** Accounts, Wallet

---
